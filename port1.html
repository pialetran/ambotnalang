Computer Science

Computer Science (CS) is a discipline that emphasizes the theoretical and scientific aspects of computing. It involves exploring fundamental questions about computation, conducting experiments, designing systems, and solving problems through programming (Denning et al., 1989). Computer Science combines both theory and practical application, enabling the development of algorithms, software, and computational models. It has a rich history as an academic field, with formal programs being offered since 1962 (Kim, 2016). The discipline continues to evolve, addressing both foundational concepts and modern technological challenges, making it essential for understanding and advancing computing.

Computing Discipline

Computing disciplines refer to the various specialized fields within the broader area of computing, each focusing on different aspects of technology, systems, and applications. According to Courte and Bishop-Clark (2009), the five recognized computing disciplines are Computer Science (CS), Information Technology (IT), Information Systems (IS), Computer Engineering (CE), and Software Engineering (SE). These disciplines are widely accepted by universities and industry standards. In recent years, new fields such as cybersecurity and data science have also emerged (Tach & Heiner, 2022). Each discipline has a specific focus: CS emphasizes theoretical and scientific aspects of computing, IT deals with computing infrastructure and user needs, IS focuses on computing in organizational and business contexts, CE merges computer science and electrical engineering to design hardware and software, and SE prioritizes reliable software system development. Together, these disciplines illustrate the diverse and evolving nature of the computing field.
