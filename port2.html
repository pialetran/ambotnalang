Data and Information

Data consists of raw, unprocessed facts and observations that lack meaning on their own. Information is created when data is organized, interpreted, and given context, making it useful for understanding and decision-making. The transformation from data to information involves processing and analysis to reveal patterns and insights. Ultimately, information empowers individuals and organizations to act with clarity and purpose.

Information System

An Information System (IS) is an integrated and interconnected set of essential components—including people, hardware, software, networks, and data—that work synergistically to collect, process, store, and distribute meaningful information within an organization. It functions as a critical bridge between advanced technology and core business operations, transforming raw data into actionable intelligence that supports day-to-day functions, enhances managerial decision-making, and aligns with long-term strategic objectives. By streamlining workflows, improving communication, and fostering innovation, an IS enables organizations to operate more efficiently, adapt to changing environments, and sustain a competitive advantage in an increasingly digital and data-driven world. Ultimately, it serves as the technological backbone that supports organizational growth, operational excellence, and informed leadership at every level.

Support System

Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)

Transaction Processing Systems handle daily business transactions such as sales, payments, and inventory. They ensure accuracy and reliability and serve as the primary source of data for other systems (Sofyan et al., 2022).

Management Information Systems (MIS)

Management Information Systems (MIS) organize data from TPS into reports that help managers plan, monitor, and make decisions. Mishra (2013) emphasized that MIS supports effective management through structured information.

Decision Support Systems (DSS)

Decision Support Systems (DSS) assist in solving complex and non-routine problems by using analytical tools and models. Amalia and Aras (2022) noted that DSS improves decision quality by allowing users to evaluate different alternatives.

Executive Support Systems (ESS/EIS)

Executive Support Systems (ESS/EIS) are designed for top-level executives and senior managers. These systems provide summarized, real-time, and visually presented information such as dashboards, charts, and key performance indicators to help executives monitor performance and identify trends. According to Clark et al. (2007), ESS supports strategic decision-making by focusing on long-term goals, external factors, and overall organizational direction rather than day-to-day operations.

Knowledge Management Systems (KMS)

Knowledge Management Systems (KMS) enable organizations to capture and share knowledge, promoting learning and innovation. Alavi and Leidner (2001) highlighted the role of KMS in making knowledge accessible across the organization.

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) integrates information from different departments into a single system, improving coordination and efficiency (Brown & Vessey, 1999).

According to Clark et al. (2007), support systems in Information Systems (IS) meet organizational needs such as decision making, process management, and performance improvement. Types include Transaction Processing Systems (TPS), Management Information Systems (MIS), Decision Support Systems (DSS), Executive Support Systems (ESS/EIS), Knowledge Management Systems (KMS), and Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP). TPS handles routine business transactions, as Sofyan et al. (2022) stated, while Mishra (2013) stated that MIS organizes information for better decisions. DSS assists decision-making with computer-based tools, Amalia & Aras (2022) noted. ESS supports executives, as Clark et al. stated. Alavi & Leidner's (2001) definition of KMS is that it manages organizational knowledge. ERP integrates cross-functional information needs, Brown & Vessey (1999) stated. These support systems, each kind has a distinct but related function in organizations. DSS and ESS assist with higher-level decisions, whereas TPS and MIS concentrate on operations and management. KMS makes learning ongoing by assisting in the collection and dissemination of knowledge. ERP ensures seamless departmental coordination by bringing everything together. This diversity demonstrates that for organizations to achieve accuracy, efficiency, and long-term growth, multiple systems must cooperate.
